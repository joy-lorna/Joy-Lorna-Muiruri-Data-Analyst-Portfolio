### 🌍 1 · OmniSphere Global Retail Profitability Deep Dive

**Excel · SQL (Google BigQuery) · Power BI**

**Business question:** In 2025, OmniSphere Global reported stable top-line revenue but is the business generating sustainable profit, or simply driving high volume at dangerously thin margins?

**Pipeline:** Six regional Excel files (data cleaning & deduplication) → Google BigQuery (UNION ALL consolidation \+ feature engineering) → Power BI (executive dashboard with scorecards, geo-maps, waterfall, drill-through tables)

| KPI | Value |
| :---- | :---- |
| Total Revenue (2025) | $4,137,375.58 |
| Total Profit (2025) | $959,345.14 |
| Global Profit Margin | \~23.2% |
| Average Transaction Value | $1,378.21 |
| Top Market by Profit | United States — $166,544.53 |
| Efficiency Champion | Electronics — 1.72% discount intensity, $60.59 profit/unit |
| Most Valuable Segment | Young Male — $124,708 profit, $1,496.91 avg order value |

**Key insights:**

- All 6 product categories are independently profitable no loss leaders detected anywhere in the portfolio  
- Discount intensity is below 2% across all categories and all markets a significant competitive advantage vs. the 10–15% industry standard, indicating strong pricing discipline  
- Margin gap across the 6 countries spans only 1.26 percentage points (US 23.89% → UK/Nigeria 22.63%), demonstrating successful global pricing standardization  
- Negative-margin transactions flagged in Vancouver, Birmingham, and Los Angeles share a common thread: unauthorized discount rates of 16–19%, roughly 10× the company average each warrants individual POS investigation  
- Young Male and Adult Female segments anchor the highest profit contribution and should lead the 2026 marketing investment strategy  
- Electronics is the portfolio's efficiency engine: highest profit per unit, lowest discount intensity, strong margin deserves a larger promotional budget share

### 🚲 2 · Bicycle Retail 3-Year Executive Sales Analysis

**SQL (SQL Server) · Microsoft Excel · Tableau**

**Business question:** How did a US bicycle retailer's sales performance evolve from 2016 to 2018 and which regions, brands, customers, and sales reps drove the most revenue?

**Pipeline:** SQL extraction from 9 normalized relational database tables (orders, customers, products, categories, brands, stores, staff) → Excel pivot tables & interactive dashboard (slicers by Year, State, Store) → Tableau executive dashboard (geographic maps, treemaps, dynamic Top-N parameters, action filters)

**Key insights:**

- New York consistently generated the highest revenue across all three years; Texas showed the lowest regional performance pointing to targeted growth opportunity questions around market size, competitive positioning, and local execution  
- Trek emerged as the dominant brand by revenue contribution, significantly outperforming all competitors warranting priority inventory management and stronger supplier partnerships  
- Top 10 customers represent a disproportionate share of total revenue, highlighting critical account retention risk and the need for a structured loyalty programme  
- Top-performing sales reps (Marceline Boyer, Venita Daniel) significantly outpaced peers their practices are candidates for formalised sales training and best-practice sharing  
- Seasonal monthly patterns identified across the 36-month window create clear opportunities for targeted promotions during slow periods and capacity pre-planning during peak months  
- The dual-tool approach (Excel for accessible analysis, Tableau for executive presentations) demonstrates a self-service analytics model that reduces analyst dependency and accelerates decisions

→ [📓 GitHub](https://github.com/joy-lorna/Bicycle-Retail-3-Year-Executive-Sales-Analysis/tree/main)

### 📱 3 · Smart Device Usage Analysis

**R, Excel, SQL**

**Business question:** How do consumers actually use smart fitness devices and what do those behavioral patterns mean for Bellabeat's product positioning and marketing strategy?

**Pipeline:** Fitbit usage dataset (public FitBit Fitness Tracker Data via Kaggle) → R (data cleaning, transformation, exploratory data analysis, visualization) → strategic recommendations for Bellabeat's marketing team

**Key insights:**

- Identified distinct activity-level segments among device users from highly active users to predominantly sedentary ones with clear implications for product messaging and feature prioritisation  
- Sleep tracking data revealed inconsistencies between recommended and actual sleep durations across user segments, signaling a content marketing opportunity around sleep health  
- Users showed higher device engagement on weekdays vs. weekends, suggesting optimal push-notification timing for Bellabeat's app  
- Correlation analysis between step counts, calories burned, and sedentary minutes provided a data foundation for Bellabeat's in-app goal-setting and coaching features  
- Analysis informed targeted recommendations: Bellabeat should position itself toward health-conscious women seeking actionable wellness insights, not just raw activity tracking data

### 👥 4 · GenTech Talent Velocity: Decoding Generational Turnover

**Microsoft Excel & Power Point**

**Business question:** Why is a fictional technology company, GenTech, losing its technical talent and are generational differences in employee behavior driving a measurable retention cliff?

**Pipeline:** HR dataset (employee records, tenure, role type, generation cohort) → Excel (data cleaning, cohort analysis, pivot tables, trend visualisation) → findings and retention strategy recommendations

**Key insights:**

- Identified a 12-month retention cliff a statistically significant spike in voluntary departures around the one-year employment mark, particularly concentrated in technical roles  
- Gen Z and Millennial employees demonstrated meaningfully different exit patterns compared to Gen X and Baby Boomers, with younger cohorts more likely to leave before the 18-month mark  
- Technical talent (engineering and data roles) showed higher attrition rates than non-technical functions, pointing to structural competitive pressure from the external talent market  
- The analysis quantified the financial cost of early-tenure attrition when accounting for onboarding, training, and productivity ramp-up investments  
- Recommendations centred on redesigning the first-year employee experience: structured 90-day onboarding, early career path clarity, and manager coaching programmes targeted at the highest-risk departure window

→ [📝 GitHub](https://github.com/joy-lorna/GenTech-Talent-Velocity-Decoding-Generational-Turnover)

### 🌱 5 · PlantCo Quantity Performance Analysis

**Power BI · DAX · Power Query**

**Business question:** How is PlantCo performing year-to-date across its international markets and which countries, products, and customer accounts are driving or dragging performance versus the prior year?

**Pipeline:** PlantCo\_data.xlsx (three structured tables: Fact\_Sales, Dim\_Account, Dim\_Product) → Power Query (type validation, deduplication, cleaning) → DAX data model (Dim\_Date calendar table, Slicer\_Values table, SWITCH measures) → Power BI interactive dashboard

| DAX Measure Category | Key Measures |
| :---- | :---- |
| Base Measures | Sales, Quantity, COGS, Gross Profit, GP% |
| Prior-to-Date | PYTD\_Sales, PYTD\_Quantity, PYTD\_Gross Profit (SAMEPERIODLASTYEAR \+ In\_Past filter) |
| Year-to-Date | YTD\_Sales, YTD\_Quantity, YTD\_Gross Profit (TOTALYTD) |
| Switch & Comparison | Switch\_YTD, Switch\_PYTD, YTD vs PYTD variance |
| Dynamic Titles | Conditional text measures updating all chart titles on slicer selection |

**Key insights:**

- Canada is the primary driver of 2024 YTD vs. PYTD gross profit decline, specifically due to Landscape product underperformance in March and April a seasonality or distribution issue warranting immediate investigation  
- February 2024 was the best-performing month, exceeding the prior-year period investigating the product mix and channel activity that month could unlock a repeatable performance playbook  
- Canada, Colombia, Croatia, and Germany are among the Bottom 10 countries by YTD vs. PYTD variance and are candidates for immediate commercial strategy review  
- A segment of customer accounts displays above-average GP% but below-average sales volume high-priority upsell targets with strong profitability potential already proven  
- The scatter chart reveals that most accounts cluster within a consistent quantity band, with a small number of outlier accounts driving disproportionately higher invoice volumes informing where account management effort should be concentrated
